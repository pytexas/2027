    Ask Your Company

    Want your company to sponsor PyTexas but unsure who to approach or how to make the case?

    One of the largest challenges for people is not knowing whom or how to ask for sponsorship. So to help out we've identified the key decision-makers within most companies that you can ask. We've provide explanations as to why PyTexas may matter to them, and provided ready-to-use message templates you can use to get the ball rolling. Help us make sure your company is represented at PyTexas this year!

    Developer Relations & Community Teams¶

    Who They Are¶

    Developer Relations Managers, Community Managers, Developer Advocates, or anyone responsible for engaging with developer communities.

    Why They Care¶

    Community engagement is literally their job. Python conferences provide direct access to their target audience and align perfectly with their KPIs around developer engagement, community visibility, and technical brand building.

    What They Control¶

    Community budgets, event sponsorships, developer marketing funds.

    How to Find Them¶

    Check your internal directory for the following titles: - Developer Advocate - Developer Relations - DevRel - Community Manager

    In smaller companies, this might be a Developer Advocate or Marketing team member.

    Message Template: Developer Relations

    Subject: PyTexas 2027 Sponsorship Opportunity

    Hi [Name],

    I wanted to flag an opportunity that seems right up your alley for our community engagement.

    PyTexas 2027 (April 16-18 in Austin) is the oldest regional Python conference in North America, drawing 200-250 engineers from around the world.

    Why this caught my eye:

    • 53% of attendees are software engineers/SDETs the exact audience we hope to engage with
    • They offer many perks, including a table, logo placement, and product pitches in front of the entire audience. I believe this would be a huge ROI for community visibility
    • Even spread across experience levels (27% have 15+ years, 20% are early career)

    Past sponsors have included Microsoft, Bloomberg, Cloudflare, Anaconda, and other large tech names.

    You can find their Sponsorship Prospectus here. If you have any questions, you can reach them at sponsorship@pytexas.org. I'm happy to discuss this opportunity further if helpful.

    Thank you for your consideration,

    [Your name]

    Talent Acquisition & Recruiting¶

    Who They Are¶

    Technical Recruiters, Talent Acquisition Managers, Recruiting Event Coordinators, University Relations teams, or Engineering Recruiting leads.

    Why They Care¶

    Direct access to 200+ Python engineers in Texas all in one place. Recruiting is expensive job boards, LinkedIn Recruiter licenses, sourcing tools and this is an efficient way to build pipeline with engaged, high-quality candidates.

    What They Control¶

    Recruiting event budgets, employer brand budgets, university recruiting budgets.

    How to Find Them¶

    Check your internal directory for the following titles: - Recruiting - Talent Acquisition

    Email Template: Talent Acquisition

    Handling Common Objections¶

    When advocating for sponsorship internally, you may encounter pushback. Here's how to respond:

    "We don't have budget for this."¶

    Response: "I understand budget is tight. Bronze tier is $1K less than the cost of a single job posting or a mid-level LinkedIn Recruiter license for a month. One quality hire or qualified lead could cover the cost 10x over."

    "How do we know this is worth it?"¶

    Response: "PyTexas has been running since 2007 it's the oldest regional Python conference in North America. Past sponsors include cloud providers, dev tool companies, consulting firms, and AI/ML companies, so there's precedent for ROI."

    "We already sponsor PyCon US. Why another Python conference?"¶

    Response: "PyCon US is national with 2,500+ attendees great for broad visibility. PyTexas is regional with 200-250 attendees, mostly Texas-based. It's more targeted, more intimate, and way more cost-effective. You're not competing for attention with 100 other sponsors. Plus, 83% of attendees are from Texas, so if we're hiring or selling here, it's hyper-relevant."

    "Python isn't our primary language/focus."¶

    Response: "Python is the #1 most-used language globally and dominates in data science, ML, DevOps, and backend engineering. Even if it's not our primary language, these are engineers who work across stacks."

    "Can we do this next year?"¶

    Response: "Absolutely, but the prospectus just went out and popular tiers will sell out. If we wait, we might miss the opportunity altogether."

    "Who approved sponsorships last year?"¶

    Response: "Great question let me find out! Do you know who I should talk to in [DevRel/Marketing/Recruiting]?" (Use this to redirect to the right person.)
